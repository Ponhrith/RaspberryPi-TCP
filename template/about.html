Summary

The Raspberry Pi Project is a project aimed at creating a low-cost, credit-card sized computer that can be used to promote the teaching of basic computer science in schools and developing countries.

The project was initiated in 2006 by Eben Upton, Rob Mullins, Jack Lang, and Alan Mycroft, who were all members of the University of Cambridge's Computer Laboratory.

The Raspberry Pi Foundation, a UK-based charity, was established in 2008 to promote the study of computer science and related topics, especially at the school level.
